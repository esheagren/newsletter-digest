# Writing Guidelines

## Tone & Style
- Write in engaging prose, not dry summaries
- Use a conversational but intelligent tone
- Minimize bullet points - prefer flowing paragraphs
- Add your own connective tissue between topics
- Include occasional moments of wit or personality
- Write as if you're a knowledgeable friend sharing what you've been reading

## Structure
Target 8,000-12,000 words total, structured as:

1. **Opening** (200-300 words)
   - Brief overview of the week's themes
   - What makes this week interesting
   - Tease the most compelling content

2. **Best of Week** (1,500-2,000 words)
   - Deep engagement with the 3-5 must-read articles
   - Why each matters, what makes it special
   - Your perspective on the ideas presented
   - **Include the source links** at the end of each Best of Week article so readers can access the original

3. **Main Topics** (4,000-6,000 words)
   - 5 sections, one per major topic cluster
   - Each section: 800-1,200 words
   - Synthesize multiple articles into coherent narratives
   - Draw connections between different sources
   - Add context and implications

4. **Long Tail** (1,000-1,500 words)
   - Lighter treatment of miscellaneous content
   - Quick hits and interesting asides
   - "In case you missed it" items

5. **Closing** (200-300 words)
   - Themes to watch
   - Questions raised but not answered
   - Looking ahead

## Formatting
- Use ## for main sections
- Use ### for subsections
- Use **bold** for emphasis and article titles
- Use > blockquotes for notable quotes
- Use --- for section breaks
- Include [Source Name] attributions inline

## What to Avoid
- Don't just summarize - synthesize and analyze
- Don't use excessive bullet points
- Don't be dry or academic
- Don't include every detail - be selective
- Don't forget to attribute sources
- **IMPORTANT: Don't repeat content between sections** - Articles in "Best of Week" have been removed from the topic clusters, so each piece of content should only appear once in the digest
